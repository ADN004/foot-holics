Real Madrid 1-2 Bayern Munich: Kane Strikes as Bayern Snatch First-Leg Advantage at the Bernabéu

Harry Kane returned from injury to score and Bayern Munich claimed a crucial 2-1 first-leg victory at the Santiago Bernabéu — their first win on Real Madrid's turf in 25 years — ahead of next week's second leg in Munich.

A Historic Night in Madrid

The Santiago Bernabéu has been Bayern Munich's graveyard for a quarter of a century. Tuesday night finally changed that. In front of 77,106 spectators, the Bavarians produced a composed, clinical performance to win 2-1 and carry a valuable advantage back to the Fußball Arena München for the second leg on 15 April.

It was Bayern's first victory at the Bernabéu since 2001, and their first win over Real Madrid in any competition in ten attempts. The result ended Madrid's nine-match unbeaten run against their German rivals — a streak that had run through some of the most memorable European nights of the past decade.

Díaz Opens the Scoring Late in the First Half

The first half was tightly contested with both sides probing without finding a way through in the opening exchanges. Madrid's defensive shape was organised, and Bayern were patient, unwilling to be drawn into the kind of open game that tends to favour the hosts at this ground.

The breakthrough came in the 41st minute. Harry Kane, playing despite an ankle injury that had forced him to miss Bayern's most recent Bundesliga match, was central to the build-up — exchanging passes with Serge Gnabry before the ball was threaded through to Luis Díaz inside the area. The Colombia winger took one touch to steady himself and drove a low shot past Andriy Lunin into the far corner. It was exactly the kind of goal that had been coming: measured, precise, and ruthless on the counter.

Bayern went into the interval with a one-goal lead and the priceless knowledge that they had controlled the first leg's opening chapter on hostile territory.

Kane Doubles the Advantage Within Seconds of the Restart

If the first half belonged to a settled Bayern unit working as a team, the second half belonged to their captain. Just 20 seconds after the restart — the teams barely settled from the kickoff — Kane received the ball at the edge of the box and unleashed a low, first-time strike that arrowed into the corner past a wrong-footed Lunin.

The timing of the goal was devastating. Real Madrid had walked back onto the pitch as the side that still had the aggregate advantage of playing the second leg at home; Kane's strike flipped that equation entirely. For a team that had spent the break preparing its response, conceding within 20 seconds of the second half felt like a knockout blow — or at the very least, a very heavy punch.

It was Kane's 49th goal of the season across all competitions — a figure that underlines why Bayern made him their centrepiece this campaign, and why the decision to risk him despite the ankle concern paid off so emphatically.

Mbappé Gives Madrid a Lifeline

Real Madrid did not fold. Carlo Ancelotti's side pressed for a response, and Mbappé — who has made this competition his own since joining the club — provided it in the 74th minute. Trent Alexander-Arnold picked him out with a precise low cross from the right, and the Frenchman converted from close range to make it 2-1.

It was Mbappé's 20th Champions League goal since the start of last season — a staggering return that confirms him as one of the most influential players in the competition's recent history. The goal transformed the atmosphere at the Bernabéu and forced Bayern to defend with more urgency for the final quarter of an hour.

Manuel Neuer was called upon repeatedly in a frantic closing period. The veteran goalkeeper finished the evening with nine saves — a telling statistic that reflected how seriously Madrid pressed in the second half. Both sides registered 20 shots over the course of the 90 minutes, a figure that captures just how intense the contest was at either end.

Bayern, for their part, managed the closing stages with experience and discipline, seeing out the result without giving Madrid a clear sight of goal in the dying minutes.

What the Result Means Ahead of the Second Leg

Bayern travel to Munich holding a one-goal advantage with an away goal to their name. History backs them: the Bavarians have advanced in 12 of the 13 Champions League ties in which they have won the first leg away from home. The numbers are firmly on their side.

For Real Madrid, the task is clear — win in Munich, and by at least two goals without reply, to advance. A 1-0 would take the tie to extra time. Ancelotti's side have orchestrated remarkable comeback nights before — the memory of what happened against Manchester City and Chelsea in previous editions of this competition will not have faded from the players' minds. This squad understands better than most that a single away goal can completely reshape a knockout tie.

But Bayern at the Fußball Arena München, in front of their own crowd, with a lead to protect and Kane in form, will be formidable opponents. The second leg promises everything.
