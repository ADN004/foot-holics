Bellingham brilliance powers Real Madrid to dominant 3-0 win over Alavés, tightening grip on title race

Jude Bellingham led Real Madrid to a 3-0 win over Alavés, helping Los Blancos strengthen their grip on the La Liga title race with a composed performance at the Bernabéu.

Bernabéu expects control as Madrid chase title momentum

The Santiago Bernabéu carried an air of expectation on 21 April 2026 as Real Madrid hosted Deportivo Alavés in a crucial La Liga fixture. With the title race entering its final stretch, every point carried weight, and Carlo Ancelotti’s side approached the match with clear intent: assert dominance early and avoid unnecessary complications. Against a disciplined Alavés side fighting to secure their league status, Madrid knew patience and precision would be required rather than sheer urgency. By the end of the night, they delivered both.

Madrid dictate tempo but face early resistance

Real Madrid controlled possession from the opening whistle, circulating the ball with confidence across midfield. Toni Kroos dictated rhythm, while Federico Valverde added vertical energy through forward runs. Alavés defended in a compact low block, limiting central penetration and forcing Madrid into wide areas. Despite territorial dominance, clear chances were initially scarce, with crosses and long-range efforts comfortably handled by the visiting defence. The match settled into a pattern of sustained Madrid pressure against organised resistance.

Bellingham breaks the deadlock with authority

The breakthrough arrived midway through the first half through Jude Bellingham, who once again demonstrated his knack for decisive contributions. Timing his run perfectly into the penalty area, he met a precise delivery and finished confidently to give Madrid the lead. The goal shifted the dynamic of the match, forcing Alavés to step slightly higher and opening spaces that Madrid had struggled to exploit earlier. Bellingham’s influence extended beyond scoring, as his movement consistently disrupted defensive positioning.

Second goal shifts control firmly in Madrid’s favour

With Alavés now required to take greater risks, Madrid capitalised on the additional space. Vinícius Júnior doubled the lead after a quick attacking transition, finishing calmly following a well-worked move that exposed gaps in the defensive line. The goal effectively removed any lingering doubt about the result, allowing Madrid to manage the tempo with greater composure. Alavés attempted to respond through counterattacks but struggled to create meaningful opportunities against Madrid’s organised defensive structure.

Game management defines second-half approach

The second half saw Madrid prioritise control over urgency. Possession was used as a defensive tool, limiting Alavés’ ability to build sustained attacks. Substitutions introduced fresh energy, ensuring intensity levels remained consistent despite the comfortable lead. Late in the match, Madrid added a third goal through a well-constructed move, further underlining their attacking depth and efficiency. The Bernabéu crowd responded with appreciation as the team demonstrated maturity in closing out the contest.

Bellingham continues to define Madrid’s season

Jude Bellingham once again emerged as the standout performer, combining technical quality with intelligent movement. His ability to influence both attacking transitions and defensive phases highlights his importance within Ancelotti’s system. Beyond his goal, his presence between midfield and attack created continuous problems for Alavés, forcing defenders into difficult decisions regarding positioning and marking. Performances like this reinforce his status as one of the most impactful players in La Liga this season.

Title race momentum remains firmly with Madrid

The victory strengthens Real Madrid’s position at the top of the La Liga table, maintaining crucial momentum as the season approaches its conclusion. Consistency in fixtures against lower-ranked opponents often defines title-winning campaigns, and Madrid demonstrated the discipline required to secure three points without unnecessary complications. Alavés, meanwhile, remain engaged in their battle for survival, needing points in upcoming fixtures to ensure safety.

Professional performance keeps Madrid on course

While not a match filled with dramatic twists, this was a performance built on control, patience, and efficiency. Real Madrid avoided complacency and executed their game plan effectively, ensuring another step toward potential domestic glory. As the finish line approaches, results like this could prove just as valuable as victories in high-profile clashes, quietly reinforcing their claim to the La Liga crown.
