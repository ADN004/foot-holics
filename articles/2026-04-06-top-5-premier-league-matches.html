Five Premier League Matches That Defined the 2025-26 Season

From an eight-goal Old Trafford thriller to a Romero bicycle kick in the 95th minute, the 2025-26 Premier League has already produced moments that will be replayed for years. Here are the five matches that stand above everything else.

1. Manchester United 5-3 Bournemouth — Eight Goals at Old Trafford

Eight goals, eight different scorers, five in the second half alone — this was the Premier League at its chaotic, breathless best. United led at half-time and looked to be cruising before Bournemouth mounted a second-half comeback that threatened to shatter a 41-year Old Trafford record: the one that says United never lose at home when leading at the break.

They did not lose it. Bruno Fernandes, with a trademark late free-kick, steadied the ship and preserved the record. But the fact that it needed preserving said everything about the drama that had unfolded. Bournemouth's refusal to accept the scoreline, their relentless pressing in the final half-hour, turned what should have been a comfortable home win into one of the most entertaining matches of the entire season. If you only watched one Premier League game this year for pure entertainment, this was the one.

2. Manchester City 5-4 Fulham — The Comeback That Almost Was

Fulham were 5-1 down at the Etihad. To most teams, the rest of that sentence would read: and they lost 5-1. Not Fulham. Marco Silva's side pulled it back to 5-4, with three goals in a frantic spell that left City genuinely rattled, and created chances in stoppage time that could have made it 5-5.

It finished 5-4 to City, but the result almost disguises the story. Fulham's performance in the second half — the way they reorganised, refused to capitulate, and dragged themselves back into a match that had seemed lost — was one of the most remarkable 45-minute shifts of the season. City were relieved to hear the final whistle. The Etihad crowd, stunned at the end of the first half, was very much awake by the time Fulham pulled it to 5-4.

3. Liverpool 2-2 Newcastle — Gordon's Red Card Changes Nothing

Liverpool had two goals and an extra man. Anthony Gordon had just been sent off for Newcastle just before half-time. The script was already written — Liverpool were going to win this comfortably. Newcastle refused to read it.

Playing the entire second half with ten men, Newcastle clawed back to 2-2 in one of the most defiant performances of the campaign. The Magpies' resilience under Eddie Howe has been a consistent theme this season, but this was something beyond even their usual determination. For Liverpool, two dropped points that felt like a defeat; for Newcastle, a point that felt like three.

4. Tottenham vs Newcastle — Romero's Bicycle Kick in the 95th Minute

Cristian Romero does not score many goals. When he does, they tend not to look like this. Tottenham's Argentine centre-back — their best defender this season — collected the ball in the Newcastle box in the 95th minute and produced a bicycle kick that bounced twice before beating Aaron Ramsdale and nestling in the net. The Tottenham Hotspur Stadium erupted.

It completed a Romero brace in a match that had already featured a controversial Anthony Gordon penalty and the kind of VAR chaos that has defined too many Premier League fixtures this season. The goal rescued a draw for Spurs — not the three points they needed given the circumstances — but as individual moments go, a centre-back scoring a bicycle kick in the 95th minute of a Premier League derby takes some beating.

5. Liverpool 4-2 Bournemouth — Salah Signs Off in Style

Hugo Ekitike opened the scoring. Cody Gakpo doubled it. Then Antoine Semenyo scored twice in 12 minutes and suddenly Liverpool's 2-0 cushion had evaporated into a 2-2 draw that nobody at Anfield had seen coming. Federico Chiesa steadied things before Mohamed Salah — ever the man for a moment — put the result beyond doubt.

The final score was 4-2, but the match had a completely different texture to what the scoreline suggests. Bournemouth's quality in that middle passage — Semenyo in particular was electric — reminded everyone that this is not a league where any side can relax. Salah's goal, and the way Anfield responded to it, was the defining image of a match that swung in every direction before Liverpool saw it out.
